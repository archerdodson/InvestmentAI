1. Purpose and Scope
This document establishes a standardised framework for the evaluation, classification, and approval of Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) transactions within Goldman Sachs Investment Banking Division. It is intended as a primary reference tool for analysts engaged in deal origination, due diligence, and advisory mandates, providing clear criteria against which each potential transaction must be assessed before progressing through the deal lifecycle.
The framework is designed to serve three core functions. First, it provides a consistent taxonomy for classifying deals by size, type, and strategic rationale, ensuring that resources are allocated proportionately to the complexity and risk profile of each transaction. Second, it establishes mandatory compliance screening gates  including sanctions, anti-money laundering (AML), anti-bribery and corruption (ABC), and ESG filters  that must be passed before any deal can advance. Third, it defines a decision mapping matrix that links deal characteristics to specific approval pathways and governance requirements.
This framework applies to all M&A transactions where Goldman Sachs acts as financial adviser, co-adviser, or principal. It covers buy-side and sell-side mandates, leveraged buyouts, mergers of equals, carve-outs, spin-offs, and joint ventures with a change of control element. It is a living document and will be reviewed annually or upon material changes to the regulatory environment.
SCOPE NOTE
This framework is intended for internal learning and training purposes within Goldman Sachs Investment Banking. It does not constitute legal advice and should be read in conjunction with the firm's compliance manuals, legal guidelines, and applicable regulatory requirements.

 
2. Framework Overview
The M&A Decision Framework is structured as a sequential evaluation process, comprising five interdependent stages that every potential transaction must pass through. The diagram below summarises the high-level flow, and subsequent sections provide detailed criteria for each stage.

4. Strategic Rationale Assessment
A compelling and clearly articulated strategic rationale is a prerequisite for any M&A transaction to advance beyond the origination stage. Analysts must evaluate the stated rationale against the client's corporate strategy, competitive positioning, and market outlook. The rationale must be specific, quantifiable where possible, and defensible to the board, shareholders, and regulators.
The following taxonomy defines the primary strategic rationale types recognised under this framework. Each transaction should be mapped to one primary rationale and, where applicable, one or more secondary rationales.

5. Compliance and Regulatory Screening
Compliance screening is a mandatory, non-negotiable gate in the M&A deal evaluation process. No transaction may proceed to financial analysis, client engagement, or due diligence until a Compliance Clearance Certificate has been issued by the firm's Compliance and Legal teams. The screening process is designed to identify and mitigate legal, financial, and reputational risks arising from the transaction.
The screening framework is organised into five pillars, each addressing a distinct category of compliance risk. Analysts are responsible for initiating the screening process by completing the Deal Screening Intake Form and submitting it to the Compliance team within 48 hours of a deal being formally originated.
5.1  Sanctions Screening
All parties to a transaction  including the target company, its ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs), key management personnel, significant shareholders, and material counterparties  must be screened against the following sanctions lists before any engagement commences:
·       OFAC Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List (SDN List)  maintained by the U.S. Department of the Treasury.
·       OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List  covering all OFAC-administered sanctions programmes.
·       EU Consolidated List of Persons, Groups and Entities Subject to EU Financial Sanctions.
·       UK Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation (OFSI) Consolidated List.
·       United Nations Security Council Consolidated List.
·       World Bank Debarred Parties List.
Screening must be conducted using the firm's approved sanctions screening software, which employs fuzzy-logic algorithms to detect name variations, aliases, and transliterations. Any potential match must be escalated to the Compliance team for manual review and disposition within 24 hours. A negative screening result does not constitute a guarantee of compliance; analysts must also assess indirect exposure through ownership structures and business relationships.
5.2  Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC)
A full KYC review must be completed for all clients and counterparties involved in the transaction. The KYC process must verify the identity of the ultimate beneficial owners, the source of funds, and the legitimacy of the business activities of the target company. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) is required for any party that is classified as a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or that operates in a high-risk jurisdiction as defined by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).
5.3  Anti-Bribery and Corruption (ABC)
All transactions involving targets that operate in jurisdictions with a Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI) score below 50 require an enhanced ABC review. The review must assess the target's compliance programme, historical conduct, and any pending or prior investigations or enforcement actions under the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act, or equivalent legislation.
5.4  Antitrust and Competition Law
For all Tier 1 and Tier 2 transactions, and for any transaction where the combined entity would achieve a market share exceeding 25% in any relevant market, a preliminary antitrust assessment must be conducted. This assessment must identify all jurisdictions where merger control filings may be required and provide an initial view on the likelihood of regulatory challenge. The assessment must be completed by external antitrust counsel and reviewed by the firm's Legal team.

CRITICAL RULE
Any transaction involving a target company, UBO, or key counterparty with a nexus to Russia, Iran, North Korea, Syria, or Cuba must be immediately escalated to the Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) and the Head of M&A Legal. The deal may not proceed under any circumstances without written clearance from both the CCO and the General Counsel. This rule applies regardless of the deal tier or the nationality of the client.

 
Analysts must also be alert to indirect sanctions exposure. A target company may not itself be domiciled in a sanctioned jurisdiction but may nonetheless present sanctions risk through its ownership structure, supply chain, customer base, or financial counterparties. The following indicators should trigger an escalation to the Compliance team:
·       Any beneficial owner holding ≥ 10% of the target who is a national of, or domiciled in, a sanctioned jurisdiction.
·       Revenue derived from sanctioned jurisdictions exceeding 5% of total annual revenue.
·       Material contracts with entities on the OFAC SDN List or equivalent lists.
·       Banking relationships with financial institutions subject to correspondent banking restrictions.
·       Subsidiaries, branches, or representative offices in sanctioned jurisdictions.
·       Significant trade flows to or from jurisdictions identified as diversion points for sanctioned goods (e.g., Turkey, UAE, Armenia, Georgia in the context of Russia sanctions).

7.2  Valuation Methodology Requirements
All deal valuations must be supported by at least two independent valuation methodologies. The primary methodology for most transactions is the Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis, supplemented by a Comparable Company Analysis (CCA) and a Precedent Transaction Analysis (PTA). For Tier 1 and Tier 2 deals, a Leveraged Buyout (LBO) analysis must also be provided as a reference point for private equity buyer interest.
·       **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF):** A 5–10 year projection period with a terminal value based on the Gordon Growth Model or an exit multiple approach. WACC must be benchmarked against sector peers.
·       **Comparable Company Analysis (CCA):** A minimum of 5 publicly traded comparable companies, with adjustments for size, growth, and profitability differentials.
·       **Precedent Transaction Analysis (PTA):** A minimum of 5 comparable transactions from the past 5 years, with adjustments for market conditions and deal structure.
·       **LBO Analysis (Tier 1 & 2 only):** Assumes a 5-year hold period and a target IRR of 20–25% for a financial sponsor.

DECISION RULE
A 'Do Not Proceed' recommendation is final and cannot be overridden by the deal team or the client. Only the Chief Compliance Officer, in consultation with the General Counsel and the Head of Investment Banking, may reverse a 'Do Not Proceed' recommendation, and only in exceptional circumstances with full written justification and board-level notification.

10.1  Escalation Triggers
The following events require immediate escalation to the Chief Compliance Officer and the General Counsel, regardless of the deal tier or the stage of the transaction:
·       Discovery of any nexus to a comprehensively sanctioned jurisdiction (Russia, Iran, DPRK, Syria, Cuba).
·       Discovery that a key party is listed on the OFAC SDN List, EU Consolidated List, or OFSI Consolidated List.
·       Receipt of a subpoena, regulatory enquiry, or law enforcement request related to the transaction.
·       Discovery of a material misrepresentation or omission in the client's representations.
·       Any indication that the transaction may be structured to evade sanctions or AML controls.
·       A request from the client to proceed with a transaction that has received a 'Do Not Proceed' recommendation.

12. Glossary
ABC: Anti-Bribery and Corruption. A body of laws and regulations designed to prevent the payment of bribes to public officials or private parties.
AML: Anti-Money Laundering. A set of laws, regulations, and procedures intended to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income.
BIS: Bureau of Industry and Security (U.S. Department of Commerce). Responsible for administering and enforcing export control laws.
CFIUS: Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States. An interagency committee that reviews foreign direct investments in U.S. businesses for national security risks.
CPI: Corruption Perceptions Index. Published annually by Transparency International, it ranks countries by their perceived levels of public sector corruption.
DCF: Discounted Cash Flow. A valuation method that estimates the value of an investment based on its expected future cash flows, discounted to present value.
EDD: Enhanced Due Diligence. A more rigorous level of due diligence applied to higher-risk clients, transactions, or jurisdictions.
EV/EBITDA: Enterprise Value to Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortisation. A common valuation multiple used in M&A.
FCPA: Foreign Corrupt Practices Act. A U.S. federal law that prohibits U.S. companies and individuals from bribing foreign government officials.
FDI: Foreign Direct Investment. An investment made by a company or individual in one country into business interests in another country.
IRR: Internal Rate of Return. A metric used to estimate the profitability of potential investments.
KYC: Know Your Customer. The process of verifying the identity of clients and assessing their suitability, along with the potential risks of illegal intentions.
LBO: Leveraged Buyout. The acquisition of a company using a significant amount of borrowed money to meet the cost of acquisition.
OFAC: Office of Foreign Assets Control. A financial intelligence and enforcement agency of the U.S. Treasury Department that administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions.
OFSI: Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation. A UK government body responsible for implementing financial sanctions.
PEP: Politically Exposed Person. An individual who is or has been entrusted with a prominent public function, posing a higher risk for potential involvement in bribery and corruption.
SDN List: Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List. A list maintained by OFAC of individuals and companies owned or controlled by, or acting for or on behalf of, targeted countries.
TTV: Total Transaction Value. The total consideration paid in an M&A transaction, including equity, assumed debt, and other financial obligations.
UBO: Ultimate Beneficial Owner. The natural person(s) who ultimately owns or controls a legal entity.
WACC: Weighted Average Cost of Capital. A calculation of a firm's cost of capital in which each category of capital is proportionately weighted.
